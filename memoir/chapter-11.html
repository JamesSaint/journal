Chapter 11 – The Aftermath (Spring 2026)

After I said, “I’m done,” there was no explosion—just stillness. Blocking her opened a space that felt unbearable at first. My body waited for the next ping, the next apology, the next hit of intermittent reinforcement. Nothing came.

That first week I wandered the house, cooked, tidied, walked the garden at midnight. Paddy stayed close. I thought about everything that had led me here: the stroke, the isolation, the dismissal by doctors, the fight to survive, the hope Jo had represented. Loving her had started to feel like erasing myself.

Grief came in waves—tenderness for the good moments, anger at the minimisations, sorrow for the time lost. I realised I had been chasing crumbs of connection from someone who could never feed me. Every withdrawal set off alarms in my body that were older than both of us.

I started writing in earnest—fragments, then pages. The more I wrote, the more I saw that this story wasn’t only about her. It was about how much I had survived without being met, and how fiercely I’d kept showing up anyway.

A few days later my phone lit with her name. I didn’t answer. Not out of cruelty, but because I finally understood that my healing couldn’t coexist with her confusion. The silence that followed wasn’t absence anymore. It was space. And in that space, something like peace began to return.
