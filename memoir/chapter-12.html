Chapter 12 – The Return to Self (Summer 2026)

By summer, something in me had gone quiet. Not numb—still. No one else’s energy in my space. No background hum of tension. Just me, this house, and Paddy.

The silence felt alien, then like oxygen. I could hear my own thoughts. Feel my own body. I walked at sunrise, letting trust grow between my mind and my nervous system. The garden became a temple. Each day I dug, pruned, watered, sat, breathed. The earth that once felt like burial now felt like belonging.

Looking back, I could trace the chapters that brought me here: selling my home; sleeping in a van; the damp walls and shattered hope; the vaccine, the stroke, the disbelief; the pills and the weaning; the garden and the fence; Rachel and Frankie; and Jo—brilliant, devastating Jo—who cracked me open and showed me where I still abandoned myself.

I reclaimed my voice by doing small sacred things: cooking a proper meal, paying a bill on time, making the bed, lighting a candle at dusk. The grief softened into gratitude. I survived what should have broken me. I still have my mind, my home, my dog, my story.

That summer I came home to myself. Not the man I was before—he’s gone. But the man who survived it all, who knows his worth not because anyone confirmed it, but because he lived through the fire and learned how to hold himself in it. There’s still pain and uncertainty. Beneath it, there’s peace—not because the world got easier, but because I stopped abandoning the only person who could ever save me: myself.
