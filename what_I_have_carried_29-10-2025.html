What I Carried

Date: 29 October 2025

I carried the house.

The cleaning. The washing. The windows. The bedding. The towels.

I carried the gardens. The lifting. The trimming. The mowing. I kept order around chaos.

I carried her car. MOT bookings. Tyres. Drop offs and pick ups. All the logistics.

I carried her comfort. Clean clothes. Stocked cupboards. Warmth. Light. Working internet.

I carried her emails and admin. Printing. Supplies. The paperwork and calls she did not want to make.

I carried her emotions. Anxiety. Self-importance. Endless healing talk.

I listened. I soothed. I waited. I hoped she would see me.

I carried her words. “Do not worry about money. Use the card.” I lived a year without buying a thing for myself.

I carried the invisible work that keeps a life running. No applause. No ledger. She took it as normal.

Underneath it all, I carried the silence. The knowledge that she was free because I held everything.

Today I set it down.

Every late drive. Every scrubbed floor. Every list and booking. This is proof of who I am. It is not a debt she can repay. It is a record of my capacity for love, discipline, and integrity.

I carried more than my share. I will not carry what is not mine again.
