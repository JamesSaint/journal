What I Carried

29 October 2025

Every small act , the washing, the folding, the hoovering basic silent offerings of love.

The invisible labour of keeping life comfortable for others: clean sheets, clean fresh towels, chaos quietly contained.

The unseen hours. The errands no one notices. The light bulbs replaced before they failed. The floors mopped before anyone saw the dirt. The comfort created without applause.

It was called “normal.” It was given no value, yet it's the fabric that holds everything together.

I carried her ease. Her routine. Her world running smoothly because I made it so. I made life soft around her edges, at the cost of hardening my own.

I carried the home like a pulse, steady, reliable, unthanked. Every act of care quietly written off as background noise. Worthless compared to an 'Executive Director' , 'A professional Leader.'

I carried the weight of being invisible. The quiet grief of giving without it being seen as giving.

I carried the order that allowed her to rest. And in return, I was told it was nothing.

But it was everything. The labour of love that kept the energy moving, the comfort possible.

Today I set it down. Not in bitterness, but in truth. Every task, every unseen act, every sacrifice, they testify to my capacity to care, to create, to hold.

It is not a debt to be repaid. It is the record of my worth. And I will never again allow what I give to be called nothing.
