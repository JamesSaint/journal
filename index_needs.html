What I Need in Our Relationship

  1. Emotional presence: I need you to attune to me emotionally, not just physically show up. I want you to notice, ask, and engage with what’s going on inside me.
  2. Mutual prioritization: I want us to treat each other’s needs and dreams with equal importance — not mine always second to yours or your kids’. I need to feel like a partner, not an afterthought.
  3. Commitment to shared growth: I need us both actively engaging in personal work — therapy, honest conversations, practices for conflict and connection. I don’t want a relationship stuck in old patterns.
  4. Respect for my chosen role: I want to contribute as homemaker, emotional anchor, and partner. I need that to be seen and valued as equal, not lesser because it doesn’t earn money.
  5. Clear financial agreements: I need us to create explicit agreements so I can spend on my needs without guilt or constant approval. I want financial autonomy within our shared plan.
  6. Erotic and relational polarity: I need our erotic life to feel intentional, alive, and safe. I want my sacred submission and bisexuality to be embraced and celebrated, not hidden or tolerated.
  7. Boundaries around legacy dynamics: I need you to acknowledge and own the impact of your relationships with your kids and ex on me. I want clear agreements on what’s healthy for us both.
  8. Shared vision: I want us to consciously create our life together — where we live, how we spend time, our financial plans, our relational agreements — rather than drifting or reacting to outside forces.
