Breaking the Silence

Date: 2025-10-31

For too long, people like me have watched our reality be managed, softened, and filed away. Today I placed my voice on the record with calm clarity. Not vengeance. Evidence.

What changed today

I sent two letters to Dr Cremona through her PA. One sets out my clinical needs and the neurological context that continues to affect my life. The other provides clinical context about Joanna that I have witnessed directly, shared respectfully, and without accusation.

I did this to end the quiet erasure of my experience. I did this to protect myself. I did this so the reality I have carried does not vanish into private pain.

Why it matters

Truth belongs in places where decisions are made. When systems only hear the tidy version, harm repeats. Putting my account into a professional frame is how I break that loop. It honours my history as a survivor. It honours the cost my body has paid since the vaccine injury and stroke. It honours the simple request that care should be reciprocal and consistent.

What I am keeping

  • My right to speak plainly without drama.
  • My boundary that care must include me, not only manage me.
  • My commitment to AXA funded, trauma informed, virtual care while my body heals.

Witness

I feel sad and good at the same time. Sad for how long this stayed unspoken. Good because I spoke it cleanly. The record now holds what my body has held. That is enough for today.
