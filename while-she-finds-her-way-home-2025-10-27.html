While She Finds Her Way Home

Written by James Saint on 2025-10-27

I am standing in the quiet aftermath. The air still carries her scent, her echo, her unfinished words. Part of me wants to run toward her, to lift her from the wreckage, to remind her we already touched what love could be. But another part of me knows: if I reach for her now, I will lift her away from the very ground she finally needs to stand on.

So I stay. I breathe. I remember that love is not ownership, and healing cannot be hurried.

  • I am not waiting, I am becoming.
  • I am not withholding love, I am protecting truth.
  • I am not losing her; I am allowing what was never whole to dissolve so that, one day, if life wills it, something truer might emerge.

I place a hand on my chest and feel the ache. It is not punishment, it is the proof of how deeply I have loved. I let it move through me like weather, not as a wound to fix, but as evidence of a heart still capable of devotion without self abandonment.

If she finds her way home, may it be as a woman who has met herself first. And if she never does, may I still be proud of how completely I stood in love and truth.
