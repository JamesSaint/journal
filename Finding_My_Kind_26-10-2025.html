Finding My Kind

Written by James Saint on 26 October 2025

There’s a strange quiet that follows clarity. Once you stop performing for approval, the noise drops away, and the landscape of your life changes. You realise how much of what you once called connection was really just compromise - the constant bending, smoothing, softening of truth so that others could stay comfortable.

Now there’s space. It’s stark at times, but it’s clean. And in that space, something new begins to take root: the knowing that I’m not meant to fit where honesty isn’t welcome.

It isn’t that there’s no one out there. It’s that my frequency has changed. The ones who speak the same language of truth and presence are fewer, but they exist - scattered, luminous, awake. I don’t need to search frantically for them. I just need to keep living in alignment so that when they see me, they recognise home.

Until then, I walk with myself (and my loyal dog). And that, for now, is enough.

James Saint
