Inner Child Re-meeting

Date: 30 October 2025

This practice was sparked by a short clip from Teal Swan that landed deeply. It names the kind of presence your pain has always needed. Not management. Not analysis. Just someone who sits with you and refuses to move on while you are hurting.

The video is below, followed by a gentle, step by step re-meeting with your younger self.

A Quiet Re-meeting

  1. Settle. Sit where your feet touch the ground. Let your shoulders drop. Breathe in through your nose, out through your mouth.
  2. Enter the room. Picture a softly lit space. On a chair sits a small version of you who learned that crying made others uncomfortable.
  3. Meet his eyes. Kneel to his level. Do not fix. Do not explain. Let your face stay open.
  4. Say the words, slowly, with breath between each line:
    I see you.
    I know this hurts.
    You do not have to stop crying.
    I am not going anywhere.
    You matter to me.
  5. If he doubts you, answer simply: “Yes. Really. I am here now.”
  6. Co-regulate. Stay until his breathing begins to match yours. No rush. No target.
  7. Return. Look around the real room. Name three things you can see, two you can touch, one sound. Feel the ground. You are here.

Notes

This practice is not about fixing the child. It is about becoming the adult who finally stays. If emotions swell, slow down and return to feeling your body on the chair or floor. You can repeat this as often as needed.
